AFRICOM

Since 2007, the United States has been sowing its colonial seeds across Africa through its security intiative, USAFRICOM. Perhaps you have heard of it, but what remains covert is how this has allowed the U.S. to exercise colonial power and exploit African nations for what seems to be the millionth time in the continent's history.

What exactly is AFRICOM?

According to the organisation itself, USAFRICOM, an acronym for for U.S Africa Command, 'provides command and control of military forces in peace and war' in order to secure the defence of African nations and, hold on, 'U.S national interests'. Critical to understanding how AFRICOM operates as a neocolonial imposition on Africa is assessing what interests the U.S. exactly has in Africa and how this causes and encourages sanctioned wars, genocides, and the exploitation of Africa's resources. AFRICOM's headquarters are in Germany and has a team of over 2000 individuals between Germany and Africa including military personnel.

Before we come onto how the U.S. is abusing its power, let's think about what AFRICOM claims it's doing in Africa. In 2010, an article by Robert Moeller was released urging readers to believe that the 'U.S. military is not trying to take over Africa' instead it details the U.S.' hands off approach to security and defence. It explains how Africa is near incapable of curtailing its own security challenges as if all 54 nations are experiencing homogenous concerns. Moeller stresses that the U.S is keeping its footprint in Africa 'limited' and that AFRICOM works to its best ability when 'listens to the concerns of its African partners'. I have linked this article if you want to read the full extent of this blatant propaganda but what this clearly constructs is an altruistic, peacekeeping image of the United States' interests in Africa. This, I must stress is not the case.

So what are the U.S government's interests? What has AFRICOM actually been doing?

Western countries have sought after influence in Africa for centuries because of the wealth of resources that exist on the continent the establishment of AFRICOM participates in this realm of interest. In short, the U.S government was dissatisfied with its waning influence on the continent and started the initiative to protect and expand its sphere of influence and hence safeguard its access to African resources.

AFRICOM's agenda, though, has been provoked by the fierce rivalry that exists between the United States and China. China's influence in Africa has been growing steadily since the mid 2000s through trade partnerships, Multinational Corporations (MNCs), and Foreign Direct Investment (FDI). The potentiality of China having better access to resources in Africa threatened the existing hegemony of the U.S' image, hence AFRICOM exists to preserve the international perception of the United States, also. Hence Africa is being used as a pawn in a new cold war between the U.S and China in which Africa bears the brunt of both superpowers' expansionist capitalism efforts.

Through expressing concerns about terrorism and civilian safety and security (which in reality is the least of AFRICOM's concern) AFRICOM has conducted airstrikes and orchestrated military coups for the past decade in order to protect and control resources vital to U.S economic interest.

But where does this leave Africa?

In true colonial fashion AFRICOM and the 29 military bases across the continent is within its right to take action against any state that they deem to be uncooperative or damaging to the U.S image or its access to resources. Notably, Muammar Gaddafi was vehemently against the formation and consolidation of AFRICOM when he was alive. It is not coincidental that he was killed just 4 years after AFRICOM was established. Gaddafi was also an advocate for Africa having full self governance and independence from Western influence. Together, AFRICOM and NATO orchestrated the conflict that resulted in Gaddafi's very public and televised death in 2011 to ensure the consolidation of US military bases across Africa without General Gaddafi's persistent opposition to the West's presence and influence on the continent. This further enabled the U.S to collude with corrupt African leaders and forward the exploitation of African resources and forward the United States' capitalist agenda for which these resources are vital.

This was the first of many instances where AFRICOM used its influenced to diminish the burgeoning power of an African government and sanction a conflict that left nations vulnerable.

The following year, AFRICOM trained soldiers lead by Captain Amadou Sanogo overthrew Mali's democratic government. Many reports lay blame on Gaddafi's use and armament of these soldiers just years before but are deafeningly silent on AFRICOM'S or the United State's role in dismantling Mali's ability to self govern in a democratic manner.

Under the guise of counter terrorism, AFRICOM has persistently increased the presence of U.S military in African nations. In West Africa, namely Nigeria and Niger, the U.S has devoted billions of dollars to counter-terrorism and surveillance efforts yet civilians are still suffering brutalities from terrorist organisations such Boko-Haram. In actuality AFRICOM is maintaining the surveillance and control over the resources the U.S government requires to benefit its own capitalist regime.

What I will draw your attention to is the persistence of AFRICOM conducted airstrikes across the continent that have ravaged towns and killed civilians near continuously over the past decade.

I hope it has become clear how USAFRICOM's presence in Africa is nothing short of neocolonialism. AFRICOM exists purely to serve U.S interest and consolidate its power and influence. Most important is its access to resources and pushing out other superpowers, namely, China. What this has resulted in is a carefully constructed and thought out version of colonialism that reinforces the infantilisation of African nations. In reality, many African nations have hardly had a chance to establish their own sovereignty because of initiatives like AFRICOM cutting movements towards democratic governance at the knees in favour of bolstering U.S wealth, power, and image. All of AFRICOM's military bases should be removed from Africa and African nations should be allowed to freely self govern in the absence of the tug of war between the U.S and China. One may question if this is too utopian or if another initiative take the place of AFRICOM and extend the reach of colonialism? Whether utopian or not the retreat of U.S militant force in Africa is a non-negotiable as the Black Alliance for Peace outlines. To ultimately deconstruct colonialism and imperialism on the African continent, it must exist in the absence of the aggressive efforts of Western capitalism, in order to ultimately come out of a position of subservience to the United States.
