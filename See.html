Not selfies but selfies. During the winter lockdown, I found myself reaching for my dslr and tripod during my saddest days. Largely, the photo library on my phone consists of my happiest memories and I’m quick to delete anything that reminds me of less than happy ones. Yet during this time of stagnation I felt the need to document how I was feeling.

depression reels 1

Canon EOS T2i 550D. The original depression reel shot in my student flat in Brixton. A place I had to escape to during the pandemic and then felt I needed to escape from. I remember trying to take some cute golden hour selfies that day and none of them came out right. I was feeling cute or candid or confident and this is what came out of that, instead. Honestly, I like it a lot more.

depression reels 2: stressy messy depressy

Canon EOS T2i 550D. Same camera, same technique. These photos were a reminder to me that I’m still beautiful when I’m sad. My hair can feel overwhelming when my emotions feel unbalanced and I really wanted to highlight how amazing it can look, matted and tangled and dry, even. I love the warmth and depth in colour in these photos. A low shutterspeed is clearly my favourite thing.
